Gold, Au

Gold (Au) is a noble metal renowned for its unique combination of chemical stability, high reflectivity, and excellent electrical conductivity. In optics, gold is often used as a thin-film coating for mirrors and other optical components, particularly in applications requiring high reflectivity in the infrared range. Its stability against oxidation and corrosion ensures long-lasting performance, making it a preferred material for harsh or sensitive environments. Gold nanoparticles have also garnered attention in the field of plasmonics, where they are used to manipulate light on the nanoscale and have found applications in sensing, imaging, and photothermal therapy.
